The Challenge

Video is heavy.
Networks are not.

Cameras on drones, phones, and security systems capture rich video. But analyzing that video requires enormous computing power — far beyond what a small device can handle. Sending the full video to a powerful server works, but on a slow network, it's painfully slow.

The existing approaches all have painful trade-offs. Either you run the heavy AI model entirely on your device (slow), send raw video to a server (eats up bandwidth), or compress the video so much that accuracy drops.

ApproxBit finds the sweet spot: it splits the work intelligently between the device and the cloud, and sends only a tiny compressed "fingerprint" of the video — not the video itself.

Real example: Recognizing what action a person is doing in a 64-frame video clip requires 38.2 trillion math operations. A modern phone can only do 2 trillion per second. That's a 16× gap — ApproxBit bridges it.

The Secret Sauce

Sending a fingerprint,
not the full video.

Instead of sending video frames or huge data tables, ApproxBit converts the AI's "understanding" of the video into a tiny string of 1s and 0s — a binary fingerprint. See how the transfer sizes compare.

Data transferred per video clip — smaller is better
ApproxBit's smallest configuration sends just 3.1 KB — that's smaller than most text emails. Yet the AI still achieves 69.2% accuracy, only 1.6% below the theoretical maximum. The competing method (DeepCOD) needs 50× more data for slightly worse accuracy.
How It Works

Three smart tricks.

ApproxBit is built on three innovations that work together to deliver fast, accurate video analysis regardless of your network quality.

🧩
Smart Splitting

The AI model is split in two. Your device handles the first part (the easy pattern recognition), and sends only a compact summary to the server for the heavy analysis. The split point adjusts automatically based on your connection speed.

🔐
Binary Fingerprinting

Instead of sending floating-point numbers (32 bits each), ApproxBit converts them to single bits — 1 or 0. This learned compression shrinks data by up to 2,142× without losing the key patterns the AI needs to make accurate predictions.

📶
Network Awareness

ApproxBit constantly monitors your connection speed and automatically chooses the best configuration — balancing how much work your device does vs. how much data gets sent — to meet your speed or accuracy target in real time.
