The Challenge

Video is heavy.
Networks are not.

Cameras on drones, phones, and security systems capture rich video. But analyzing that video requires enormous computing power — far beyond what a small device can handle. Sending the full video to a powerful server works, but on a slow network, it's painfully slow.

The existing approaches all have painful trade-offs. Either you run the heavy AI model entirely on your device (slow), send raw video to a server (eats up bandwidth), or compress the video so much that accuracy drops.

ApproxBit finds the sweet spot: it splits the work intelligently between the device and the cloud, and sends only a tiny compressed "fingerprint" of the video — not the video itself.

Real example: Recognizing what action a person is doing in a 64-frame video clip requires 38.2 trillion math operations. A modern phone can only do 2 trillion per second. That's a 16× gap — ApproxBit bridges it.
